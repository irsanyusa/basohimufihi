8 German YouTubers to elevate your German skills

 Posted on May 25, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  973 words  |  Martina Birk

YouTube videos can be a very useful part of your language-learning process. And there’s no shortage of options when it comes to YouTubers who can help you learn the German skills you need. 

But with so many choices, how do you know which German YouTuber will actually give you the boost you need to reach your language goals? 

Well, you’re in the right place. Below, we present to you the best YouTube channels to learn German—whether you’re an absolute beginner in need of some survival German or an advanced speaker who just wants to brush up. 

  • Learn German with Anja
  • Easy German
  • Get Germanized
  • Learn German with Herr Antrim
  • Deutsch für Euch
  • Girls4Teaching: German with Eva
  • Lingoni German
  • My German Short Stories

    1. Learn German with Anja

    Cheerful YouTuber Anja describes herself as “a teacher from Germany who teaches German in an un-German way.” 

    With energetic and entertaining videos that keep learners motivated, Learn German with Anja is one of the most popular YouTube channels for German lessons. 

    Anja creates short, simple videos focused on narrow topics (for example, “How to say ‘My name is…’ in German”), and breaks down grammar points into easy-to-digest pieces. German vocabulary is presented as on-screen text so viewers can follow along easily.  

    2. Easy German

    Created by duo Janusz and Cari, the popular Easy German channel teaches learners how German is spoken “in the streets.” 

    The hosts interview German speakers in the streets of Berlin and talk about a wide range of topics, from “Why Germans love cycling” to “How to introduce yourself in German.” Each video features both German and English subtitles for learners to follow along. These videos are super useful for those who want to hear “real” German in context.  

    They also produce a weekly podcast where hosts Cari and Manuel discuss all things about the German culture. Members their Patreon community get acccess to interactive tanscript, vocabulary helper and bonus content so listeners can learn German on-the-go.

    3. Get Germanized

    YouTuber MeisterLehnsherr has chosen “Live, laugh, learn” as the motto for his channel Get Germanized. 

    He’s been introducing learners to German culture and language for nearly 12 years and posts new videos every Saturday. 

    Covering beginner to advanced levels, Get Germanized deals with cultural comparisons, travel videos and more. Fans of this channel enjoy MeisterLehnsherr’s facial expressions, use of humor, easy-to-follow pace and repetition of new words and expressions. 

    4. Learn German with Herr Antrim

    Herr Antrim is a teacher with more than a decade of experience, offering hundreds of language learning videos on his channel Learn German with Herr Antrim. 

    This entertaining channel offers clearly laid-out lessons and tons of visual aids, so it’s fantastic for beginners (but also caters to all levels). 

    There’s new content every week dealing with a wide range of subjects, such as grammar and vocabulary, common issues, expressions and practical tips.

    5. Deutsch für Euch

    On her channel Deutsch für Euch, self-proclaimed “goofy girl” Katja offers German lessons for all levels. 

    In each five- to ten-minute video, she provides extensive and accessible explanations of grammar, listening comprehension activities, cultural discussions and more. 

    Katja teaches from the perspective of an English speaker and makes connections between English and German. She’s known for her knack for explaining complex concepts clearly. Her videos also include a random word of the week (RWW), helping viewers build their vocabulary. 

    6. Girls4Teaching: German with Eva

    Aimed at A1 learners, German with Eva is part of the Girls4Teaching channel. 

    Eva’s lessons include grammar, vocabulary, culture and listening. The course is divided into units of ten lessons with a progress test at the end of every unit. 

    German with Eva features a blackboard graphic where Eva presents German words alongside their English translations. She also offers a few videos that are entirely in German for those who want a challenge.

    7. Lingoni German

    Previously known as “German with Jenny,” Lingoni German is geared toward A1 to B2 learners. 

    Having spent several years in the United States, Jenny is very skilled at giving helpful English explanations for German concepts. 

    Using situational language (at the airport, in the office, etc.), Lingoni German’s easy-to-follow videos allow learners to practice listening comprehension, learn grammar structures and grow their vocabulary. 

    8. My German short stories

    Suitable for levels B1 to B2, My German Short Stories is a departure from your standard German lessons. 

    The channel features videos that are each about seven minutes long, with clear titles like “A story about wishes” and “A story about competition” so viewers know what to expect. 

    The short stories are told in German alongside helpful German on-screen text, illustrations and photos so viewers can follow along.  

    Bonus! Simple Germany

    Simple Germany is a great resource for understanding life in Germany. It consists of a blog and a YouTube channel created by Jen and Yvonne, two women living in Düsseldorf – Jen from Guatemala and Yvonne from Germany.

    Their channel gives expats in-depth information and tips about living in Germany, bureaucracy, culture and services

    Learn with the best German YouTubers

    Just like apps, websites and movies, YouTube videos can be a useful part of your language-learning plan. 

    The YouTubers featured here will get you excited about German, no matter what your level is or what you’re interested in. Check them out today!
