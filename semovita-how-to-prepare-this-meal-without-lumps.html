Semovita: How to prepare this meal without lumps

 Posted on June 12, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  212 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Semovita is a brand name for one of the many soft gelatinous substances called "swallow" in Nigeria. It is usually part of the main course, served as an accompaniment for Nigerian soups, and is prepared by continuous boiling.

Cooking time: 15 minutes

Nutritional value: High in carbohydrate. One cup of Semovita yields 600 calories.

ADVERTISEMENT

Recipe Yield: Serves 2 people

Ingredients

Semovita (or any other brand of your choice)

Wooden spatula for stirring

ADVERTISEMENT

Instructions

1. Pour about 500ml of water into a clean pot and leave to boil on high heat.

2. Take a bowl, pour a cup of Semovita in it and add some cool water into the bowl. Use the wooden spatula to stir the water and Semovita continuously until a smooth consistency is achieved.

3. When the water in the pot is boiling, reduce the heat of the stove to medium heat and then, pour the mixed Semovita into the boiling water.

4. Continue stirring for 1-2 minutes and then allow to cook on low heat for about a minute.

ADVERTISEMENT

5. Stir again until the texture is consistent and then turn off the heat.

Your Semovita should be perfect and ready to serve! Serve with Onugbu soup, Egusi soup, or any other soup of your choice.
